# Chrome Circuit Cowboys — Street-Level RP Sprawl (PROPOSAL)

**~250 roleplay rooms** on the same floor as Neon Alley — a grind-optional social
sprawl where RPers can run campaigns without fighting through the level system.
10 districts × 25 rooms. Most rooms are RP-safe; light **L1–11** mobs appear only
where the vibe calls for it (and cluster in The Gutters). No franchise IP —
generic cyberpunk throughout. Currency is **scrip (€$)**; medics are **Emergency
Medics (EMs)**.

> **This is a proposal — nothing is carved yet.** Review the map + this manifest,
> then tell me what to cut/add/rename and I'll build the rooms.

**Tags:** `RP-safe` · `light-mob:<type>` · `vendor` · `EM` · `fixer` · `club` ·
`bar` · `food` · `lodging` · `transit` · `junk-vendor` · `data-port`

Each district connects to The Strip / Neon Alley through a single **ENTRY** room,
and has its own internal **LAYOUT** (hub / spine / grid). Two **transit loops**
(see below) also ring the sprawl so you can travel district-to-district without
backtracking through The Strip — like Houston's 610 and Beltway 8.

## 1. The Strip & Avenues — the connective spine
*Neon canyons, crowd-choked sidewalks, holo-ad spillover, transit spurs. Links every other district.*

1. **The Grand Concourse** — Ten lanes of foot-traffic beneath a ceiling of layered holo-ads, the air thick with steam-vendor smoke and overlapping sound systems; rain sheets off the overhangs in glowing curtains. `[RP-safe, transit]`

## Transit Loops + Express Spokes — the road network

The layout is **concentric**: The Strip sits at the center, two **roleplay-backdrop
ring roads** wrap around it, and the **10 districts hang on the OUTSIDE** of the
outer beltway. The fast way around is radial, the scenic way is the loops:

- **Express spokes (fast travel):** 9 radial routes fan straight out from The Strip
  to each district, crossing both loops at on/off-ramps. This is how you get from
  the center to any block quickly — pick a district, ride its spoke out. Short.
- **The two loops (RP backdrop):** the Inner Circuit and the Sprawlbelt are *not*
  the fast path — they're wander-able rings of roleplay rooms between the spokes,
  there for atmosphere and circumferential travel (district-to-adjacent-district)
  rather than speed. Houston-style: 610 inside, Beltway 8 outside.

### The Inner Circuit — elevated express (fast, ~22 rooms)
An elevated neon **maglev ring** that circles close to the core. Short hops:
**2–3 transit rooms per leg** between district stations. The quick way around —
hum of the rail, ad-glare streaking past the windows, the city a blur below.

- **9 legs**, one between each pair of adjacent districts (Neon Bazaar → Watering
  Holes → Pulse → Velvet Quarter → Gutters → Wharf/Old Town → Chrome Alley →
  Noodle Row → Verticage → back to Neon Bazaar), 2–3 rooms each ≈ **22 rooms**.
- Sample leg rooms (flavor): *Skyrail Platform 7* `[transit]` · *The Humming Span*
  (a glass tube over the avenues) `[transit]` · *Maglev Car 12* (a packed express
  car, strangers swaying) `[transit, light-mob:pickpocket]`.
- Station rooms double as quick meet-ups; mostly **RP-safe**, the odd pickpocket.

### The Sprawlbelt — outer beltway (long, ~68 rooms)
A ground-level **beltway** of overpasses, off-ramps, and frontage strips ringing
the whole city edge. Long hauls: **5–10 transit rooms per leg** — the scenic,
lived-in route past the sprawl's backsides, useful as RP backdrop in its own right.

- **9 legs**, 5–10 rooms each ≈ **68 rooms**, each themed to the two districts it
  bridges (e.g. the Gutters↔Wharf leg runs grimy and fog-wet; the Noodle
  Row↔Verticage leg is warm frontage stalls and tenement underpasses).
- Sample leg rooms (flavor): *The Frontage Strip* (rain-lashed off-ramp diners)
  `[transit, food]` · *Underpass Camp* `[transit, light-mob:junkie]` · *Beltway
  Toll Plaza* `[transit, light-mob:ganger]` · *The Long Off-Ramp* `[transit]`.
- Carries a few more **light mobs** than the inner loop (it skirts the rough edges)
  but is still overwhelmingly traversable RP space.

> Exact per-leg room names get written at carve time — for now the structure is:
> **inner = 2–3/leg express, outer = 5–10/leg scenic, both full rings of 9 legs.**

## Recurring Fixtures — Junk Vendors & Data Ports

Two things get **sprinkled through every district** (and along both backdrop loops)
so the sprawl always has texture for roleplayers, whatever block they're on.

### ▣ Junk Vendors — low-priced trinkets (`junk-vendor`)
Roving hustlers, blanket-on-the-ground sellers, and coin-op vending walls hawking
cheap **RP props** — €$1–20, no combat value, pure flavor for a scene (a costume
piece, a prop to bluff with, a mysterious shard to build a plot around). At least
one wanders each district's entry/transit room; the Neon Bazaar is wall-to-wall
with them. Sample stock:

| Trinket | Price | Roleplay hook |
|---|---|---|
| Cracked chrome shades | €$3 | look cool, see nothing in the dark |
| Dented dog-tag (someone else's name) | €$2 | instant mysterious backstory |
| Fortune-cookie chip | €$1 | prints a cryptic one-liner |
| Worn deck of cards | €$5 | start a game in any bar |
| Rusted spark-lighter | €$4 | still throws a flame |
| "Lucky" circuit-amulet | €$6 | does nothing, allegedly |
| Faded band patch (defunct synth-act) | €$3 | wear your subculture |
| Keychain drone-toy | €$8 | buzzes in dumb little circles |
| Unlabeled mystery data-shard | €$10 | maybe junk, maybe a whole campaign |
| Souvenir mug ("I ♥ THE STRIP") | €$2 | the perfect bad gift |
| Tangle of salvaged cable | €$2 | always useful, somehow |
| Knock-off synth-perfume vial | €$5 | smell like money you don't have |

> Mechanically these can be a tiny no-stat "trinket" item class (hold/show/give/
> drop) so they're cheap to add and never unbalance combat — they exist to be
> *roleplayed with*, not equipped.

### ⌁ Data Ports — public jack-in terminals (`data-port`)
Physical **jack-in points** where a runner plugs a cyberdeck and drops into the Net
(the L11–99 Net path) from meatspace — then climbs back out at the same port. This
directly fixes the flagged *"netrunner can't reach the Net"* gap: with a public
port in **every district**, a netrunner RPer is never far from a way in, solo or
as a crew. Flavors vary by neighborhood:

- **The Strip** → *The Rain-Curtain Arcade* (16): a clean public info-kiosk port.
- **Neon Bazaar** → *Bootleg Software Stand* (11): a sketchy grey-market tap.
- **Verticage** → *The Pirate Hub-Den* (18): jack into a pirated tower signal.
- **Noodle Row** → *The Bubble-Tea Den* (8): a café "deck counter," time by the hour.
- **The Gutters** → *Snakebite Den* (15): a risky back-alley tap (watch for ICE).
- **Velvet Quarter** → *The Whisper Salon* (24): a discreet, private port.
- **The Pulse** → *The Dream Den* (13): a jack-in lounge of contour-pods.
- **The Watering Holes** → *The Dead Drop* (6): port wired beside the mail-canisters.
- **Chrome Alley** → *Wetwire Optics* (19): a clean clinic-grade terminal.
- **The Wharf & Old Town** → *Customs Shed 3* (8) + *The Net-Loft* (11): smuggler taps.

Plus coin-op **jack booths** (old-payphone style) scattered on the transit spurs and
loop stations, so you can dive in mid-journey.

> Net-path build (the L11–99 jack-in world) is tracked separately — these ports are
> the *meatspace doorways* into it, placed here as RP entry points.
